Cooling is one of the largest ongoing costs at a telecom site. The good news:
thoughtful design choices — many of which we build into every remanufactured
shelter — can meaningfully lower energy use without risking uptime.

## Start with the envelope

A well-insulated, well-sealed shelter keeps conditioned air where it belongs. That
reduces how hard the HVAC has to work, especially through temperature swings
between summer and winter.

## Modern, variable-speed HVAC

We equip shelters with **variable-speed HVAC systems** using the latest
refrigerants, tuned for optimal winter and summer performance. Variable-speed
compressors ramp to match the actual load instead of cycling hard on and off —
using less energy and extending equipment life.

## Redundancy done efficiently

Configurations like **lead-lag control** balance runtime across units and provide
automatic backup, so you get both reliability and efficiency. For higher loads,
multiple units on a PLC controller can stage on only as cooling demand requires.

## Lighting and monitoring

Energy-efficient **LED lighting** and a complete alarm package with high/low
temperature sensors round out the picture — cutting draw and catching problems
before they become outages.
