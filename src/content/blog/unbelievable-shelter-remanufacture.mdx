When this project arrived, few would have guessed it could be saved. The starting
point was a severely dilapidated **12' x 30' telecom shelter** — part of the
interior roof had collapsed, and both the interior and exterior showed significant
cosmetic damage. But the concrete infrastructure underneath remained structurally
sound, and that's exactly the kind of shelter remanufacturing was made for.

## The requirement

The customer needed part of the shelter converted into a **generator room** on a
tight timeline and budget. That meant reconfiguring the interior, adding access,
and providing proper airflow for the generator — all while restoring the structure
to like-new condition.

## What we did

- Partitioned the interior to separate telecom racks from the generator
- Added **two new door locations** on one side of the shelter
- Repurposed the original door opening as an **air-intake louver** for the gen room
- Installed a new energy-efficient **polyvinyl roof** backed by a 10-year workmanship warranty
- Applied concrete sealant, re-caulked all seams, and finished with an industrial-grade paint matched to the customer's exact color specification

## The result

A structurally sound, like-new shelter — restored with more durable materials than
are often found on brand-new units, and delivered on schedule. It's proof that
remanufacturing isn't a compromise; done right, it's an upgrade.
